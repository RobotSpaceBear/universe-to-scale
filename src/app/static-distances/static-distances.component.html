Distances explained
Light seconds The light-second (ls) is a unit of length useful in astronomy.
It is defined as the distance that light travels in free space in one second, and is equal to exactly 299 792 458 metres.

Light year The light-year (ly) is the distance light travels in a year, which is equivalent to about 9.46 trillion kilometers.
You can calculate that distance by multiplying the number of seconds in a year by the distance of a light second.

Example :
365 days x 24 hours per day x 60 minutes per hour x 60 seconds per minute x 299 792 458 metres per second = 9 454 254 955 488 000 metres.
Or about 9.46 trillion kilometers.

Solar System The Sun's light takes, on average, 500 seconds to reach the Earth, or 8 minutes and 20 seconds.
It takes 2597 seconds for light to travel from the Sun to Jupiter.
Pluto is, on average, 5.3 light-hours away from the Sun. That's over 19 000 seconds !

Outisde the Solar System The closest star to the Solar System is Alpha Centauri. It is only 4.37 light-years away, or about 137 717 712 light-seconds away.
Hercule's Cluster (M13), a star nursery of a few hundreds of thousands of stars, is 25 000 light-years away.
The closest galaxy to the Solar System is Andromeda. It is only 2.54 million light-years away, or about 80.1 trillion light-seconds away.
